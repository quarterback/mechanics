Institute for Public Mechanics

Public Mechanics is the practice of understanding how systems function, fail, and get repaired in real-world conditions.

What is Mechanics?

Mechanics is the work of maintaining and adjusting systems at the point where they meet people. It focuses on consequence, repair, and the conditions that allow systems to keep working over time.

This includes the technical, the procedural, and the institutional. Mechanics practitioners work inside delivery teams, government agencies, infrastructure projects, and service organizations where complexity and consequence intersect.

The Institute

The Institute for Public Mechanics supports this field through research, frameworks, and professional development. We work with practitioners to articulate methods, document approaches, and advance the discipline.

Our focus is on applied work: how systems operate under pressure, how they break down, and how they can be designed for durability and care.

We plan to host events, publish writing, and convene the community of people already doing this work.
